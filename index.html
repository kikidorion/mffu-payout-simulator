How to read this honestly. The output is only as good as your inputs — a curve-fit backtest in produces a fantasy curve out. Three traps the sliders exist to model: (1) most backtests are gross — commission and slippage were never subtracted, and across hundreds of trades that quietly eats a large slice of edge; (2) negative-skew profiles (high win rate, avg loss > avg win) die by loss clusters, not averages — a $2,000 trailing drawdown can be tripped by one bad morning even when the long-run expectancy is positive; (3) funded accounts use tighter intraday trailing drawdown than the eval's EOD math, so an account that passed comfortably can still get clipped on the funded side.
